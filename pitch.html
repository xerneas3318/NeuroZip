03Where + when the meaning lives

We compressed 144-fold — throwing away everything but meaning — and looked at what survived. It's not smeared across the brain. It concentrates at occipitotemporal electrodes, biggest right at the N170, 150–200 ms after stimulus — the window the brain identifies objects. We used compression as a microscope.

05How it works · one sentence

We put a frozen judge in the loop that scores whether the meaning survived, and train the compressor against it — so the model that decides what to keep is what lets you search by typing.
EEG epoch ──► encoder ──► quantize ──► decoder ──► EEĜ ──► P (frozen judge) ──► ε̂
                                                                                                    ↑ gradient flows back
                                                                                                      1 − cos(ε̂, CLIP_image(seen_image))

L = λrate · bits + λrecon · ‖EEG − EEĜ‖² + λtask · (1 − cos(ε̂, ε*))
P's weights stay frozen; the gradient does not. That's the only trick. The same model that scores "did the meaning survive" at training is the model that takes your free-text query at inference. See ARCHITECTURE.md for the full derivation, the three correctness conditions, and the v1 → v4 evolution.

06Where we're weak — and why we trust it anyway

We'd rather you trust these numbers than be dazzled by them, so here's the honest read.
Subjects
1
sub-01 of THINGS-EEG's 10. Pipeline is subject-agnostic, but we haven't run multi-subject. Cross-subject is the next overnight job.
Trials
trial-averaged
80 reps per concept, averaged. Single-trial decoding is harder; we don't claim to solve it. Codec contribution would carry; absolute numbers would drop.
Retrieval edge
+4–9 pp
Not +50. Honest magnitude. At iso-rate; bigger gaps when you don't control for bits.
And we tried to catch ourselves cheating. The retrieval metric runs through the same projector we trained the codec against — that's the circularity objection a sharp judge will raise. So we trained a separate classifier on a different EEG split with a different loss and a different output head, and never optimized the codec against it. At 144× compression it reads NeuroZip with 100% and fidelity with 88%. That 12-point gap is measured against a judge whose only relationship to training was that the codec was never asked to satisfy it. The win survives in the regime where compression actually matters.
